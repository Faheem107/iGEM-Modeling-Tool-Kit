Introduction: why are we rejecting prong 3?

1. Sodium alginate is highly water-absorbent. As a hydrogel, it may retain a significant amount of water, potentially reducing water availability and limiting nutrient diffusion to our bacterial cells, which could negatively affect Prong 2\.   
2. A consistent Ca²⁺ supply is essential to promote calcite formation. Without sufficient calcium, the bacteria may produce alternative calcium carbonate polymorphs or poorly formed mineral aggregates, compromising the intended bioremediation outcome. (as it will make the sand cement…)  
3. While it's effective, it isn't a synbio solution. Since one of the main goals of iGEM is to demonstrate engineering through genetic design, having one of our three core prongs rely solely on an externally added material may make our overall system seem less integrated. Replacing it with a genetically encoded strategy would better showcase our engineering contribution. 

What is gerB\*?

Prong 3: Engineer spores to germinate more readily by overexpressing **gerB\***, allowing them to transition more quickly into metabolically active vegetative cells that can establish biofilms and carry out the desired function.  

* Dormant spores do **nothing**.  
* Only vegetative cells produce EPS/biofilm and perform our engineered functions.

→  If spores germinate faster or more uniformly, you should get **more active cells earlier**, which could translate into earlier biofilm establishment before environmental stress becomes too severe.  
Overexpression of the mutant germinant receptor GerB\* is expected to lower the threshold for nutrient-triggered germination, promoting faster and more synchronous conversion of dormant spores into metabolically active vegetative cells. 

The pathway for the suggested prong 3 will be:  
GerB\* overexpression  
↓  
faster/more uniform germination (which also tackles the issue of spores staying dormant\!)  
↓  
earlier vegetative growth  
↓  
earlier biofilm production  
↓  
biofilm may improve persistence under desert conditions \+ requires less media (which solves the issues of nutrient deficiency in the desert)

So:   
Prong1: Improvement (overexpressing an already existent gene)  
Prong2: Addition (adding CA to synthesize calcium carbonate)  
Prong 3: Control (making sure all bacteria performs the wanted functions)

And since our village is bioremediation, we can frame the project along the lines of:  
Function: Promote rapid biofilm formation to optimize desert bioremediation, which may decrease biofilm formation duration time and, therefore, reduce risks of sand erosions on population.   
Containment: Eliminate vegetative cells using a genetically encoded kill switch.  
Long-term Biosafety: Prevent the formation of second-generation spores by combining enhanced germination with genetically controlled inhibition of re-sporulation.
